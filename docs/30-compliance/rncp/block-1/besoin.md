## Required Documents

The dossier must include the following items to validate competencies **C1 to C5**.

1. **Needs Analysis (User Research)**
   - Evidence of exchanges with client/users (interview notes, survey or questionnaire results).
   - Accessibility treatment for people with disabilities must be explicitly described from this phase.
2. **Current-State Audit**
   - Technical, functional, and security audit report of the current environment.
   - Current technical architecture modeling.
3. **Specifications (Main Part of the Dossier)**
   - Functional specifications describing user needs and usage scenarios.
   - Technical specifications describing the solution with testable and independently deliverable parts.
4. **Financial Analysis and Benchmark**
   - Benchmark of comparable market solutions.
   - Budget estimate (build and run costs) across multiple scenarios.
5. **Risk and Evolution Analysis**
   - Forward-looking migration and evolution study.
   - Operational chain modeling with potential failure points.

## Role of the Prototype

The prototype should support explanation and feasibility evidence, not replace the framing work of Block 1.

- Use the prototype to justify architectural and technical decisions.
- Keep focus on framing, scope, rationale, and cost assumptions.
- Avoid over-investing in implementation details that belong to later blocks.

---

## Oral Defense Guidance (M1)

The defense typically includes **10 to 20 minutes** of presentation, followed by **20 to 30 minutes** of discussion.

- Prepare a clear accessibility section (frequent evaluation point).
- Explain the 2-3 year project evolution path in simple terms.
- Be ready to justify audit and needs-gathering methodology.
